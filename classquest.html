ClassQuest

Background

What is ClassQuest?

ClassQuest is an application built to promote student attendance and in-class engagement by gamifying the learning process. Sometimes students need a little push to get to and stay in class. ClassQuest accurately tracks attendance by using the location of a student's phone to make sure they are in class. Once the student reaches the location of their class, the app will allow them to sign into the lecture session with a code provided by the instructor. It will also continue to monitor the student's location and time spent in class. An in-app RPG game lets the student collect coins and defeat monsters (every time they come to class and answer questions) to encourage students to engage with the material and their peers. Additionally, students can access their previous participation questions to review the class materials and give their teachers feedback after every lecture.

Ideation

Brainstorming

We wanted to create an application for HackDavis that addressed an issue in education. After brainstorming some ideas, we went back to our own experience as students and noticed that there is a lack of attendance and participation in our classes, despite there being many applications and consoles that check class participation in schools and colleges. We wanted to try and solve this issue in a creative way that was easy and economical for both students and teachers. Currently devices that check attendance, like iClickers and Tophat, are expensive and easily bypassed. They also do not offer any other incentive to be used.

User Research

Defining Core Users

As we were working within a very short time frame, we used observational data to generate simple personas for our 2 core users. The first user is a student who does not have the motivation to go to class unless they are forced to do so - they usually have a friend sign in for them on the attendance sheet. The second user is a student who goes to class, but who easily loses focus on the lecture and ends up not retaining any of the content that was taught.

Creating Layouts

Wireframing

Using the ideas we generated during our brainstorming session, we sketeched simple wireframes on a chalkboard and iterated on different layouts and features that we wanted to include in our final prototypes.

Deliverables

Final Protoypes

We used Adobe XD to create the UI mockups using graphics from Freekpik.com and Flaticon.com which we edited with Adobe Photoshop and Illustrator. Based on the mockups, we created the app in Xcode using Swift and added the functional elements.
