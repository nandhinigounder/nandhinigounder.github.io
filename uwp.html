University Writing Program Website

Background

What is UWP?

The University Writing Program (UWP) department at UC Davis offers a variety of writing courses and related services. Every undergraduate student at UC Davis takes a course (or equivalent) with the UWP department at some point during their education. Therefore, it is vital that information regarding these courses and services are easily accessible to anyone who visits the website. As of January 2018, the UWP website contained a multitude of outdated information on an interface that was visually displeasing and difficult to navigate. As part of my internship with Dearadh Consultancy, my fellow interns and I worked together as a cross-functional team in order to revamp the website and bring it into the 21st century.

User Research

Data Collection

We first decided to leverage our connection with the UC Davis population in order gather as much information and opinions as possible. Using Qualtrics, a survery design and analysis software, we created a survey that asked questions designed to gain a general idea of what people thought about the website. We moved the 66 responses we received into Excel and organized them – grouping similar responses. Following this, we contacted UWP faculty and staff through listserv and interviewed those who were willing to participate in order to get more feedback. We also interviewed friends and peers to round out our more in-depth interviews.

Research Analysis

Defining Core Users

By breaking down the data and analyzing responses for similarities, we were able to define 5 core users of the UWP website. We created detailed personas for each user, paying close attention to pain points and use cases.

Content Organization

Site Flow

One insight we gained from our users was that certain information was diffcult to find because they did not know where it was located. In order to tackle this problem we re-examined the site flow and general organization of content. We discovered that there was frequent redundancy between site levels and the placement of information under certain topics did not make sense. Additionally, there were numerous broken links. In order to gain a better understanding of the content, we created a makeshift sitemap using post-its. We removed redundancies and simplified the site flow so that most information could be accessed within 3 clicks.

Testing Layouts

Wireframes

We used Balsamiq to create low-fidelity wireframes. We then tested these wireframes with users, asking them to find key information and making any necessary adjustments based on the results.

Identifying Realistic Solutions

User Constraints

A key element of this process, was creating a realistic implementation and maintenance plan for the redesigned website. An issue that we ran into with this was when we found out that the current webmaster, who also designed the site, was not on board with revamping the website. As he did not wish to speak with us, we had to find an alternative method that would allow a member of the UWP department (with no prior experience in web development) to implement and maintain the new website. While we were brainstorming and researching different ideas, we discovered that UC Davis has a website building software, called Sitefarm, specifically created for departments within the university. SiteFarm is built for those who are not familiar with web development and features an intuitive drag-and-drop interface, tutorials, training, and techinical support. After speaking with the UWP department, we decided that using SiteFarm would be the best course of action.

Visual Elements

Updating the Logo

In order to fit the minimalistic theme of SiteFarm, we redesigned the UWP logo. We stayed in tune with the original style, but modernized it with the sans serif font and the more vibrant shades of blue and yellow.

Style Guide

Fonts

In order to stay consistent with UC Davis branding, we used the official font - Proxima Nova. We utilized different variations of Proxima Nova to separate catergories and establish information heirarchies.

Style Guide

Color Palette

Once again, in order to stay consistent with UC Davis branding, we used the official color palette published by the university - UC Davis Blue, UC Davis Gold, and black. All other colors are either different opacities of the official colors or built into the SiteFarm Template and cannot be changed.

Deliverables

Final Prototype

After multiple iterations and testing we produced our final prototype (right). For comparison, the original UWP website is shown as well (left). We simplified the navigation and made sure to make prominent quicklinks for the most viewed content. We also added blocks for contact information as well as upcoming events.

Deliverables

Additional Screens

In addition to the homepage, we prototyped two screens - Courses and People. These screens contained the majority of the most accessed information and showcased the two other general layouts. All other pages on the website will follow the same layout as one of the two screens.

Conclusion

Reflection

I had a great time working with my team over the course of this internship and this project. While we faced some unexpected challenges, I believe we were able to navigate them effectively. Our final deliverables and presentation were met with enthusiasm from the members of the UWP department — who are excited to have a brand new website!
